1876

The first antecedents of the radio date from the 19th century, with the invention of the voltaic battery by Alessandro Volta. From that moment the first telegraphs began to be built, which evolved thanks to the contributions of Samuel Morse, with the Morse code.

In 1876, Graham Bell was the first British scientist who discovered that sounds could be heard through a cable. But it was not until the twentieth century, that Fleming and Fessenden, achieved the transmission of the human voice, an issue that originated the beginning of that medium.

Prior to the birth of the radio devices, an investigation was carried out based on the electromagnetic waves of James Clerk Maxwell, which were validated by Heinrich Rudolf Hertz through a device through which he showed how to create electromagnetic waves and the process to detect them. However, it was Marconi, who thanks to his knowledge, allowed the sound signals to be propagated up to 20 km, a great achievement for the time and for the evolution of this medium, the radio.
