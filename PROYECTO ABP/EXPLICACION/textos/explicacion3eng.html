AM / FM

A program is transmitted by type of wave called carrier, this is called Modulation. We can add a radio program to the carrier wave, thus causing the signal to produce fluctuations in that wave. This whole process is known as FM frequency modulation.

What is the difference between AM and FM?

The first is known as Modulated Amplitude and was the first one used to make radio. The AM is below the FM modulation, so it has always been tried to improve the transmission quality, this is the reason about the FM was born. All this is summed up in what we call Bandwidth: While the AM frequency has a bandwidth of 10KHz, the FM has a frequency of 200KHz. The AM frequency has a large wavelength and travels long distances, but the FM has a lower wavelength, however it has a higher speed so that we can send signals in stereo. For this reason, we see that the music channels are broadcast by this signal, and the radio channels by the AM signal.
