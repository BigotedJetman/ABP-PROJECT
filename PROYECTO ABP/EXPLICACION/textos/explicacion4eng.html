Radio Components

The main components of the radio are the following:

Antenna: It is the responsible for receiving and broadcasting the signals by air. The signals that the antenna receives are copies of a signal that is being transmitted, and that are sent to a diode.

Coil: It allows synchronizing specific radio signals, making it possible for us to choose certain transmission frequencies while we ignore the signals we don't want. When we tune a radio in different stations, what we are actually doing is modifying the coil inside the radio.

Diode: It is the part of the Radio that processes the signal that picks up the antenna. Its operation is like that of a switch, allowing the electric current to go in one direction capturing the signal we want, and in turn, blocking the signals we do not want in the other.

Speaker: It is the part of the radio that converts the signal captured by the antenna and it processed by the diode, converted into audio so that listeners can listen to it. This is transmitted in a sound wave that mimics the original audio transmission, with the difference that our ear can interpret it, being able to decipher its content.
